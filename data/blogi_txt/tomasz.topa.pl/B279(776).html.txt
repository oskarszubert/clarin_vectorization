Nie jest. Jak już pisałem wcześniej, nikt do końca nie wiedział kiedy to ma ruszyć. Dzisiaj na forum polskiego eBay-a pojawiła się nowina: PayPal ma obsługiwać polską walutę od 12 października. W przeciągu kilku godzin PayPal „will be performing maintenance” i „site may be unavailable during that time”. Niektórzy wierzą jeszcze, że coś w tym czasie z nami zrobią, ale według aktualizacji regulaminów, raczej nie ma co liczyć na rewolucyjne zmiany przez 12 października:  Amendment to the User Agreement and policies Notice Date: September 14, 2006 Effective Date: October 12, 2006 Beginning October 12, 2006, PayPal will support the following currencies as part of the PayPal service: The Swiss Franc, Norwegian Krone, Swedish Krona, Danish Krone, Polish Zloty, Hungarian Forint, Czech Koruna, Singapore Dollar, Hong Kong Dollar and New Zealand Dollar. The User Agreement and associated policies will be amended to reflect the availability of payment processing in these currencies. Also, if a user is registered in a country which has as its official currency one of the additional currencies added to the service, that currency will become that user’s home currency. Amendment to the PayPal Buyer Protection Programme Notice Date: September 14, 2006 Effective Date: October 12, 2006 Beginning October 12, 2006, the scope of the PayPal Buyer Protection programme will be expanded to allow the terms of the programme to apply to sellers who list on the applicable PayPal Buyer Protection eligible eBay sites and who are registered PayPal users in the following countries: Denmark, Sweden, Greece, Portugal, Finland, Norway, New Zealand, Argentina, Brazil, Mexico, Uruguay, Chile, Ecuador, Jamaica, Poland, Czech Republic, Hungary, Thailand and China. PayPal User Agreement available in Polish Notice Date: September 14, 2006 Effective Date: October 12, 2006 Beginning October 12, 2006, the User Agreement will be made available in Polish and registered users in Poland will become subject to the terms of the Polish language User Agreement.
